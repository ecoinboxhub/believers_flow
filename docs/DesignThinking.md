# Design Thinking - Believers Task Flow

## 1. Empathize
Believers often feel overwhelmed by the conflict between secular productivity and spiritual discipline. "Busyness" is often seen as an obstacle to prayer and study. The goal is to create a tool that doesn't just manage "work" but integrates "walk."

## 2. Define
The problem is that existing task managers are sterile and secular. They focus on efficiency but ignore spiritual balance. Users need a way to see their spiritual growth as part of their daily "task flow."

## 3. Ideate
- **The "Balancer"**: A UI element that shows the ratio of spiritual vs. secular tasks.
- **Micro-Devotionals**: Bible verses that appear when a task is completed.
- **Gamified Prayer**: Tracking "Prayer Balance" like a resource in a game to encourage consistency.
- **AI Shepherd**: Using an LLM to look at a busy day and suggest a 5-minute prayer pause or a specific verse.

## 4. Prototype
- **Minimalist Web App**: Using Vanilla JS for speed and offline capability.
- **Android WebView**: The most efficient way to deliver a mobile experience without the overhead of heavy frameworks.
- **Color Palette**: 
  - `Heavenly Blue` (#3A7BD5) for calm.
  - `Holy Gold` (#F2C94C) for highlights and achievements.
  - `Purity White` (#FFFFFF) for background and clarity.

## 5. Test (Initial Assumptions)
- Users will value the offline nature of the app (no distractions from notifications).
- The "Prayer Balance" feature will be the most used spiritual tool.
- GROQ integration will provide "Believer Activity" ideas that feel personal and relevant.
